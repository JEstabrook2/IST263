Discover Frye Island

Welcome to Frye Island, a unique seasonal retreat nestled in the expansive Sebago Lake, Maine's second-largest lake. Spanning approximately 1.5 miles in width and 2.5 miles in length featuring an area of 1.34 miles, the island offers a peaceful getaway with stunning natural landscapes, accessible only via a ferry from Raymond.

Wildlife and Natural Beauty

Frye Island is renowned for its diverse ecosystems and the scenic beauty of its surroundings. The island is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts, featuring native species such as ducks and deer freely roaming the woodlands and shores.

Activities and Recreation

The island's life is vibrant with an array of recreational activities tailored for every age and interest. Whether you're a sports enthusiast or seeking relaxation, Frye Island has something for everyone.

  • Boating and Fishing: Engage in boating or cast your fishing line into the rich waters of Sebago Lake, known for its excellent bass and salmon fishing.
  • Water Sports: Enjoy water skiing, paddle boarding, and swimming in the crystal-clear lake waters.
  • Golfing: Challenge yourself at the well-maintained nine-hole golf course that offers both enjoyment and panoramic views of the lake.
  • Tennis and Beach Sports: Play a match on the island's tennis courts or join beach volleyball games held on the sandy shores.
  • Community Events: Participate in the variety of events hosted at the community center, ranging from workshops to festive seasonal gatherings.

Seasonal Accessibility

It's important to note that Frye Island is accessible only part of the year. Ferry services operate from spring through late autumn. As winter sets in and the lake freezes, the island becomes a tranquil, snow-covered haven, largely inaccessible until the thaw of spring.
